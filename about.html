MARK RICHARD ROBINSON WRITING · ART · THEOLOGY

ABOUT

A life shaped by breath, church, sketchbooks, and stubborn hope.

I serve as a minister, writer, and artist, working with congregations and communities who live close to the edge — close to illness, uncertainty, injustice, and grace.

Ministry and work

My days move between church halls, hospital corridors, and station platforms. I preach, listen, sit in meetings that decide the future of buildings, and stand alongside people whose lives carry more weight than most policies ever admit.

The work on this site grows out of that ministry: sermons that turn into chapters, prayers that return as poems, sketchbook lines that become prints and covers. None of it is detached from real congregations and real streets.

Writing, art, and theology

I write in the overlap between Black theology, liberation theology, womanist insights, and the everyday faith of people who keep showing up even when the world makes that difficult.

The drawings stay simple on purpose: boats, hands, railway lines, shoes. Each line tries to honour those whose stories rarely make it onto gallery walls or front pages. I believe God is present in those stories, sharing breath and bearing weight with us.

What you’ll find here

You will find a novel that sits with illness and endurance, an art project that sits with dangerous journeys, and a journal that gathers small pieces of reflection, prayer, and resistance. Everything here is offered as conversation, not conclusion.

If any of this meets you where you are, you are welcome to carry it into your own work, your own church, your own acts of quiet refusal and hope.
