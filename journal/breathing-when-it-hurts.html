Breathing When It Hurts

Illness · Survival · Breath · Hope

Somewhere between the monitor’s slow pulse and the rustle of plastic curtains, breath turns into a decision. The machine counts each inhale. The body weighs every exhale. The room holds quiet conversations that never reach the nursing station.

In that space, prayer changes shape. It becomes smaller and more stubborn. Less about outcomes, more about presence. Less about endurance in theory, more about the courage to remain with what the body is carrying.

The God who once felt distant and clean now lives at the end of a cannula, in the patience of a nurse who adjusts a pillow, in the kindness of a friend who sits through visiting hours without reaching for easy words.

Breathing when it hurts is a costly agreement to remain. Each inhale declares, quietly and fiercely: “I am still here.” And in that stubborn act of staying, God shares the labour of each breath.
