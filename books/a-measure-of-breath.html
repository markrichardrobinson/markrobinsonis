Novel

A Measure of Breath

A novel of fragile lungs, fierce love, and the cost of staying alive.

Set among trains, hospital wards, and London streets, A Measure of Breath follows the lives of people whose days are shaped by monitors, oxygen, and constant calculation of risk. At the centre sits a family learning how to carry grief, rage, faith, and love without losing one another.

What the book sits with

Breath and illness. Immigration and empire. The quiet heroism of nurses and cleaners. The weight churches carry when they face injustice directly. The story is grounded in hospitals and estates rather than abstract debates.

The page holds moments of laughter, fury, tenderness, and stubborn kindness alongside spiritual crisis. Faith here breathes in the spaces between appointments, ventilators, and bus journeys.

How it connects with the other work

A Measure of Breath lives within a wider body of work: drawings, sermons, prayers, and the ongoing Waters Between project. These pieces share questions of exile, risk, solidarity, and the unwavering presence of God in hard places.

The novel can stand alone, or it can accompany the visual and devotional work across this site. Everything grows from the same soil.
