Pulse: A Devotional in Fragments

Small readings for days shaped by hospital corridors, church services, and the long work of hope.

Pulse is a devotional project in development, gathering short reflections, prayers, and poems written in the middle of real life: in waiting rooms, at train stations, in church meetings, and beside beds where sleep does not come easily.

Hear when it is ready

Shape of the project

The pieces in Pulse are short by design. Many are written for people who live with fatigue, with limited breath, with little spare time or energy. Each reading aims to carry enough depth to matter, and enough lightness to be held in tired hands.

Some entries sit close to scripture. Others sit close to lived experience: clinics, housing meetings, family conversations, church disputes, and small acts of mercy that rarely receive attention.

How it connects with the rest

This devotional grows alongside the novel A Measure of Breath, the Waters Between artwork, and the journal on this site. They all share the same conviction: that God is present in vulnerable bodies, contested spaces, and communities searching for justice.

When the project is ready, details will be shared here and through the journal and mailing list.
