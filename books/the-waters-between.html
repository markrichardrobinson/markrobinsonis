Art Print · Project

The Waters Between

A graphite meditation on risk, exile, and the courage to cross dangerous waters.

The fisherman rows a small boat out from a familiar shore toward something that may either save him or destroy him. The piece draws on stories of migration, perilous crossings, and biblical images of water, storm, and call.

Order a print Read the poem

Drawing and process

The artwork began as a series of loose graphite sketches: a figure in a boat, nets falling into deep water, the shape of a distant shoreline. Each version stripped away detail until only the essentials remained.

The simplicity is deliberate. It leaves space for viewers to bring their own crossings, separations, and reunions into the scene.

Where this project sits

The Waters Between sits alongside the novel A Measure of Breath as a companion image. Both works wrestle with empire, the limits of the body, and the determination to keep moving.

The project will continue through further drawings, poems, and reflections that hold together risk, justice, and hope.
